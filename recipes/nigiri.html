Tasty Salmon nigiris

Ingredients

For the sushi rice

  • 1 cup Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1¼ cups water
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

For the nigiri

  • 200–250 g sushi-grade salmon (very fresh)
  • Wasabi (optional, traditional)
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)

Equipment (helpful, not mandatory)

  • Very sharp knife (yanagiba if you have one)
  • Rice paddle or wooden spoon
  • Damp kitchen towel

Instructions

  1. Make the sushi rice
    1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs mostly clear.
    2. Cook with 1¼ cups water (rice cooker or stovetop).
    3. While hot, transfer to a wide bowl.
    4. Gently fold in the vinegar, sugar, and salt using a slicing/lifting motion (don’t mash).
    5. Cover with a damp towel and let cool to body temperature.
  2. Prepare the salmon
    1. Keep the salmon cold until cutting.
    2. Slice against the grain into pieces about:
      • 5 - 6 cm long
      • 2 - 3 cm wide
      • ~5 mm thick
    3. Each slice should drape naturally over rice—not stiff, not falling apart.
  3. Shape the nigiri
    1. Wet your hands lightly (water + a little rice vinegar).
    2. Take ~1½ tablespoons of rice.
    3. Gently press into an oval—light and airy, not compact.
    4. Dab a tiny bit of wasabi on the salmon (optional).
    5. Lay salmon over rice and press gently with two fingers.
    6. Flip once, press lightly on the sides, done.

    You’re aiming for three gentle presses total—that’s it.

  4. Serve
    • Serve immediately.
    • Dip fish-side down into soy sauce (not rice-side).
    • No need for extra wasabi if you used it inside.

Pro tips

  • If you’re new to slicing fish, partially freezing the salmon for 10–15 minutes helps.
  • Warm rice + cold fish = perfect texture contrast.
  • Less rice than you think. Nigiri should feel light, not filling.
