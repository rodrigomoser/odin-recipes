Tasty Salmon nigiris

Ingredients test

For the sushi rice

  • 1 cup Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1¼ cups water
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

For the nigiri

  • 200–250 g sushi-grade salmon (very fresh)/li>
  • Wasabi (optional, traditional)
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)

Equipment (helpful, not mandatory)

  • Very sharp knife (yanagiba if you have one)
  • Rice paddle or wooden spoon
  • Damp kitchen towel

Instructions

  1. Make the sushi rice
    1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs mostly clear.
    2. Cook with 1¼ cups water (rice cooker or stovetop).
    3. While hot, transfer to a wide bowl.
    4. Gently fold in the vinegar, sugar, and salt using a slicing/lifting motion (don’t mash).
    5. Cover with a damp towel and let cool to body temperature.
  2. Mix the ricotta layer

    In a bowl, combine ricotta, egg, Parmesan, salt, and pepper. Set aside.

  3. Cook noodles (if needed)

    Boil lasagna noodles in salted water until just al dente. Drain and lay flat.

  4. Assemble the lasagna

    Preheat oven to 375°F / 190°C.

    In a 9×13 baking dish:

    • Spread a thin layer of sauce
    • Add noodles
    • Spread ricotta mixture
    • Add meat sauce
    • Sprinkle mozzarella

    Repeat layers until everything’s used, ending with sauce + mozzarella + Parmesan.

  5. Bake

    Cover with foil (tent it so cheese doesn’t stick).

    Bake 25 minutes covered, then 20 minutes uncovered until bubbly and golden.

  6. Rest (important!)

    Let it sit 10–15 minutes before slicing. This keeps it from collapsing into lasagna soup.

Optional upgrades

  • Add sautéed mushrooms or spinach to the sauce
  • Swap beef for Italian sausage (or half & half)
  • Use béchamel instead of ricotta for a more Italian-style vibe
